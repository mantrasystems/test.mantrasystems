What is MDR Compliance?

Unless you are an expert in medical device regulation, achieving MDR compliance could be a real challenge. Our team of MDR medical experts are here to help.

MDR compliance is the status of complying with all relevant aspects of the Medical Device Regulation (MDR) 2017/745. After May 2021, it will be necessary for all medical device manufacturers to demonstrate MDR compliance of the devices they make.

Learn more about MDR compliance requirements by arranging a free consultation with a member of our team.

Requirements for MDR Compliance

MDR Compliance, as a minimum, has the following requirements:

  • Produce and manufacture a medical device according to established safety and quality processes
  • Demonstrate conformity with the Annex I General Safety and Performance Requirements (SPRs) through clinical evidence, after performing a Gap Analysis
  • Plan, document, implement, and maintain a Quality Management System (QMS)
  • Design and implement a Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) system for each device
  • Design and implement Post-Market Clinical Follow-up (PMCF) for every device
  • Produce a dossier of Technical Documents
  • Document and implement a Risk Management process
  • Undertake a Clinical Evaluation for every medical device and document the evaluation in a Clinical Evaluation Report (CER)
  • Have a Person Responsible for Regulatory Compliance either within the organisation or permanently at its disposal

Rules for assessing MDR Compliance differ according to the risk class of the device. For Class IIa, IIb and III devices a Notified Body will be required to perform an assessment of the device, associated technical files, and surveillance, quality, and risk management systems to determine whether MDR Compliance has been achieved.

Although Class I devices do not normally require involvement of a Notified Body, it is still necessary to produce technical files and develop appropriate quality, Risk Management, PMCF and Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) systems to monitor device performance.

MDR compliance is demonstrated by the application of a CE mark, either self-applied in the case of Class I devices, or following receipt of a certificate of conformity from a Notified Body for devices in other risk classes.
