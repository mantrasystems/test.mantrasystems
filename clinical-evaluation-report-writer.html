Engaging a Clinical Evaluation Report (CER) writer can future-proof your business

A professional Clinical Evaluation Report (CER) writer performs an essential function in ensuring ongoing regulatory approval for medical devices. A Clinical Evaluation Report details the process, findings and conclusions of clinical evaluation and must be produced for every medical device under the MDR regardless of risk class.

A CER is a complex technical document. A clinical evaluation report writer should have a proven ability to understand and appraise clinical evidence, have relevant therapy area expertise, and a high level of familiarity with the clinical evaluation procedure in MDR Annex XIV Part A. Working with a professional clinical evaluation report writer with appropriate experience and skill can be a big step towards a successful MDR transition.

What MDR rules must a Clinical Evaluation Report writer adhere to?

Article 61 of the MDR states that the Clinical Evaluation of each medical device must be documented in a Clinical Evaluation Report as part of the technical documentation portfolio for the device. MDR Annex XIV Part A expands upon this requirement and provides detailed guidance for performing a clinical evaluation, stating that every CER must:

  • identify all clinical data relevant to the device, whether favourable or not
  • appraise clinical data using a robust, objective and documented methodology
  • identify any gaps in the clinical evidence portfolio
  • reach objective conclusions about the safety and performance of the medical device
  • make a determination of the benefit-risk profile of the device accounting for all available evidence

The EU Commission document MEDDEV 2.7/1 rev 4 contains further guidance that every clinical evaluation report writer should adhere to. MEDDEV 2.7/1 rev 4 has a useful section on CER structure and content but the document has not yet been updated to reflect changes in CER requirements introduced by the MDR. Because of this, a clinical evaluation report writer that adheres to MEDDEV 2.7/1 rev 4 alone, without working with the MDR directly, will not achieve compliance with the MDR.

What happens to a Clinical Evaluation Report after completion?

Following completion, a Clinical Evaluation Report will be assessed as a component of the technical documents required by Annex II MDR to be submitted in support of the device. The procedure by which this assessment will take place will vary according to the risk classification of the device, with greater regulatory scrutiny being required for higher risk devices.
